Before the Call

Show Up Prepared.

You don't need everything in order. Most clients come with one faded certificate and a rough memory of when it was bought. That's enough to start. Five minutes of prep saves a lot of back and forth on the call.

01
Find your certificates.

Physical certificates, old passbooks, dividend warrants — anything with a company name on it. Even one is enough. Don't worry if it's faded or partially illegible.

→Check old files, lockers, or stored documents
02
Write down the company names.

Note the names printed on the certificates. Don't try to look them up first. Bring the name as-is. We trace company mergers and name changes on the call.

→Faded text? A phone photo under good light works
03
Know the rough time period.

Roughly when were the shares bought — 1980s, 90s, early 2000s? A decade is fine. No need for exact dates. The certificate print date is usually visible if you look closely.

→Approximate decade is enough to start
04
Clarify whose name the shares are in.

Your name, a parent's name, or a spouse's? If the original shareholder has passed, note whether there was a nomination registered with the company.

→This determines direct claim vs. legal heir case
05
For NRIs: note your country of residence.

This affects documentation requirements and the account type for demat credit. Nothing to prepare in advance — just knowing this on the call helps us give you accurate timelines.

→No India travel required in most NRI cases
